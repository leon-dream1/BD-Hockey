Junior Program

Junior hockey programs typically cater to players in various age groups, ranging from as young as 6 or 7 up to 20 years old. The age groups are often divided into categories like Under-8, Under-10, Under-12, Under-14, Under-16, and Under-18.

Teenager Program

Teenager programs typically cater to players in the age range of 13 to 18 years old. The specific age categories may vary depending on the club or league structure.Similar to junior programs, teenager programs focus on refining and advancing players' skills.

Professional Program

Professional programs are typically for players who have progressed through junior and teenage programs and have demonstrated a high level of skill, talent, and potential. These programs may include players in their late teens and early twenties.Professional programs are led by experienced and highly qualified coaches, often with backgrounds in professional hockey.
